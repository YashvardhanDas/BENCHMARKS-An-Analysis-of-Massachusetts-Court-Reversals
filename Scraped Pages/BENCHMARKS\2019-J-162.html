04/29/2019 ORDER: The stay of proceedings on the plaintiffs' petition is vacated. To obtain relief from an interlocutory order, the petitioner faces a substantial burden. Edwin R. Sage Company vs. Foley, 12 Mass. App. Ct. 20, 25 (1981)( Single justice relief under G. L. c. 231, § 118, first par., is granted in a "stinting manner with suitable respect for the principle that the exercise of judicial discretion circumscribes the scope of available relief."). Successful petitioners must demonstrate, in their petition and supporting filings, that the challenged order is the product of a clear error of law or an abuse of discretion. Jet-Line Services, Inc. v. Board of Selectmen of Stoughton, 25 Mass. App. Ct. 645, 646 (1988).

In their petition, the plaintiffs argue that the trial court judge erred in her description of the uncontested facts surrounding plaintiff Diane Giammasi's accident in the defendant's store. However, whether the judge's description of the placement of the stocking cart is inaccurate in her memorandum and order on the summary judgment motion is immaterial in light of her conclusion that the placement of the cart is not regulated by the building code.

The petitioners also argue that the judge's conclusion that the placement of the stocking cart does not violate the building code as a matter of law is error. In support of their position, the plaintiffs are unable to cite and I am not aware of any appellate case establishing that a wheeled cart left in an aisle way by an employee could constitute a violation of the building code. In the absence of any such authority, it cannot be said that the trial court judge committed a clear error of law warranting intervention by a single justice of this Court. See Aspinall v. Philip Morris Companies, Inc., 442 Mass. 381, 390 (2004)("[a trial court] judge's order should ordinarily not be overturned if it is . . . unaffected by clear error of law.")

The plaintiffs' petition is denied. (Blake, J.). Notice/Attest/Kazanjian, J.
