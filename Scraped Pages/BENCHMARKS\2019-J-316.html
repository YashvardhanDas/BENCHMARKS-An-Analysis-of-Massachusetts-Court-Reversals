07/09/2019 RE#1:The mother has filed a petition, pursuant to G. L. c. 231, § 118 (first para.), seeking review of a temporary order dated 06/27/2019 and entered on the Probate and Family Court's docket on 07/01/2019. Mother and father share joint legal and joint physical custody of the parties' two minor children. The children spend each week alternating between the parents according to the judgment of divorce and as agreed by the parties. Sometime after the parties' divorce, father relocated to Rhode Island, apparently without seeking leave to remove the children from the Commonwealth. At some point thereafter, mother relocated to Connecticut, likewise without seeking leave to remove the children from the Commonwealth. The challenged order states "Neither party shall remove the minor children . . . from the Commonwealth of Massachusetts." The order later states "Failure of the mother, Amineh A. Archer to return the children to said Commonwealth of Massachusetts may result in imposition of sanctions for violation of the orders of the Court." It is unclear from the record before me as to why this provision singles mother out for possible sanctions when the record before me suggests that both parties removed the children from the Commonwealth without leave. I note that mother has filed a motion for further temporary orders, which is currently scheduled to be heard on 08/12/2019, and may provide the Probate and Family Court judge with an opportunity to clarify whether and how both parties should return the children to the Commonwealth. Pending the outcome of that hearing, or any earlier hearing held by the judge to clarify the temporary order, so much of the temporary order requiring the mother or the father to return to Massachusetts from Connecticut or Rhode Island, respectively, is hereby stayed. Because this temporary stay is being ordered prior to the expiration of the time in which father may respond by right to the mother's petition, father may move to vacate the temporary stay at any time during the pendency of the stay. Proceedings on the mother's petition to this court are stayed pending the outcome of the hearing in the Probate and Family Court. On or before 08/19/2019, the mother shall file a status report with this court in which she shall report the outcome of the 08/12/2019 hearing, or any earlier hearing, in the Probate and Family Court. Failure to file a timely status report may result in the stay of the temporary order being vacated. So ordered. (Shin, J.). *Notice/Attest/McMahon, J.
