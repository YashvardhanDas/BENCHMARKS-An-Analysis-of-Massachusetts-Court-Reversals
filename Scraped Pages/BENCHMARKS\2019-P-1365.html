09/16/2019 #1 Lower Court Assembly of the Record Package
09/16/2019 Notice of entry sent.
09/16/2019 Entry fee waived per 2019-J-405.
09/16/2019 ORDER: The lower court docket indicates that judgment entered on 6/11/18. Pursuant to M. R. A. P. 4(a), the notice of appeal was due within 30 days of the entry of judgment unless a timely motion is made or served under the Massachusetts Rules of Civil Procedure and filed with the lower court. After judgment entered, the defendant apparently filed with the lower court a motion for reconsideration although the docket does not reflect the date of that filing. The lower court docket reflects that the motion was denied on 1/4/19, after which the defendant filed on 1/31/19 a second motion for reconsideration. It was not until 2/25/19, following the lower court's denial of the defendant's second motion for reconsideration, that the defendant filed a notice of appeal in which she stated she was appealing from the 6/11/18 judgment. It appears the notice of appeal was late. Accordingly, on or before 9/25/19, the defendant is ordered to move for voluntary dismissal of the above-captioned appeal pursuant to M.R.A.P. 29 (b), or to show cause, in writing, why jurisdiction over this appeal is proper. *Notice/attest
09/23/2019 #2 Status Report, filed by Doreen Scolastico Riley.
10/02/2019 ORDER: Counsel for appellee has advised that he was not served with the status report filed on 9/23/19 ( paper no. 2) nor did appellant file a certificate of service . Accordingly, appellant is to serve counsel forthwith with a copy of the status report and file a copy of the certificate of service with the court, and file and serve all future filings in compliance with Mass. R. App. Proc. 13 . Upon service of the status report, appellee shall have ten days to file any response to the court's order of 9/16/19 and appellant's filing. *Notice/Attest

10/28/2019 #5 MOTION to extend brief & appendix due date, filed for Doreen Scolastico Riley.
11/15/2019 RE#2: As the defendant's response does not demonstrate that her notice of appeal was timely filed, the within is treated as a motion for leave to file a late notice of appeal and is denied without prejudice to renewal accompanied by the required showing of (1) excusable neglect, i.e., the reason that the notice of appeal was not timely filed and (2) a meritorious appellate issue, i.e., the legal arguments, with citation to legal authority indicating that the Superior judge's order is in error. See Tisei v. Building Inspector of Marlborough, 3 Mass. App. Ct. 377, 378-79 (1975). The renewed motion must be filed on or before 11/27/2019. (Sacks, J.). *Notice.
