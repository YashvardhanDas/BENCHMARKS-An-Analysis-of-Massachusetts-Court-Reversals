10/11/2018 ORDER: After a jury verdict for plaintiff was returned on May 17, 2018, the defendant City of Malden ( "Malden") filed a Motion for New Trial, to Alter or Amend Judgment, or for Judgment Notwithstanding the Verdict on June 7, 2018 ( Paper No. 41)While the post-judgment motions were pending, judgment entered on June 14, 2018. Malden filed a Notice of Appeal on July 12, 2018. On August 23, 2018, and after hearing, the trial judge entered an order denying, in relevant part, Malden's "Post-Verdict Motions" (Paper No. 48). No subsequent notice of appeal was filed. The record was assembled on August 28, 2018, and Malden entered the appeal on September 6, 2018, on Appeals Court docket no. 18-P-1251.

Mass. R. App. Proc. 4 provides that "[i]f a timely motion under the Massachusetts Rules of Civil Procedure is filed in the lower court by any party:(1)for judgment under Rule 50(b)...(3)to alter or amend a judgment under Rule 59...or(4)under Rule 59 for a new trial, the time for appeal for all parties shall run from the entry of the order denying a new trial or granting or denying any other such motion. A notice of appeal filed before the disposition of any of the above motions shall have no effect." Accordingly, it appears that the notice of appeal filed on July 12, 2018, was premature and a new notice of appeal should have been filed after the order of August 23, 2018, disposing of the post-judgment motions. See Anthony v. Anthony, 21 Mass. App. Ct. 299, 302 (1985) ("As to all appeals to which new Mass.R. App. 4(a) applies we shall give unqualified effect to the language of the new rule, i.e., an appeal founded on a notice of appeal filed prior to disposition of a postjudgment motion under Mass. R.Civ. P.50(b), 52(b), or 59 is a nullity and shall be dismissed.").

Accordingly, on or before October 25, 2018, Malden is to move for voluntary dismissal of the appeal pursuant to Mass. R. App. Proc. 29(b), or show cause, in writing, why jurisdiction over this appeal is proper, or file a motion pursuant to Mass. R. App. Proc. 14(b) for leave to file a late notice of appeal. Failure to take action by that date may result in dismissal of the appeal for failure to prosecute. . *Notice/Attest
