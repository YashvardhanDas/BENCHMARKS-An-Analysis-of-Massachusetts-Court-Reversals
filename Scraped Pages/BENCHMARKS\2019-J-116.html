04/09/2019 REVISED ORDER RE#1: The defendants South Washington Street, LLC and 849 South Washington Street, LLC, seek review of an order entered in the Hampden Superior Court on January 28, 2019. The petition was filed on March 15, 2019, more than thirty days following entry of the order. The thirty-day time limit is established by G. L. c. 231, § 118, ¶ 1; it is jurisdictional and may not be enlarged. The filing of a motion for reconsideration in the lower court does not enlarge or create a new thirty-day time period. McGrath v. McGrath, 65 Mass. App. Ct. 670, 671 (2006). Moreover, an order denying a motion for reconsideration, unless a substantially new or newly-supported motion, does not provide a separate basis for relief under G. L. c. 231, § 118, ¶ 1. Id. at 672. See also Selby Associates v. Boston Redevelopment Auth., 27 Mass. App. Ct. 1188, 1189-1190 (1989); Manousos v. Sarkis, 382 Mass. 317, 322-323 (1981).

Review of the two motions presented to the lower court reveals no substantial difference between the relief sought or the grounds relied upon. Accordingly, insofar as the petition seeks review of the original order, it is untimely. Insofar as it seeks review of the denial of the motion for reconsideration, I discern no abuse of discretion on the part of the lower court judge. All relief requested in the petition is denied. (Blake, J.). *Notice/attest/Mason, J.
