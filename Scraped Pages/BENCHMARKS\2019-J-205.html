05/15/2019 #5 Response to petition for interlocutory relief filed for Samuel Goethals and Mary Goethals by Attorney Howard Miller.
05/20/2019 ORDER: This matter involves subsequent proceedings after our decision in Bruno v. Zoning Board of Appeals of Tisbury, 93 Mass. App. Ct. 48 (2018). On remand, the Land Court judge received further briefing and then entered a "Remand Order" in which he remanded the matter to the Tisbury Zoning Board of Appeals, so that the relevant Tisbury officials "may in the first instance locally weigh the equitable factors implicated here, and decide on appropriate remedies for the violations of the zoning bylaw dimensional requirements." Petitioner argues that the remand order is contrary to law and must be vacated, because only the court, not the local zoning authorities, has the power to consider "equitable factors" under G. L. c. 40A, §§ 7 and 17. I do not read the remand order, however, as in some way delegating the court's power to determine equitable remedies to the local authorities. Under section 17, the judge has the power to "make such [a] decree as justice and equity require." It appears to me that in exercising that power, the judge can solicit the views of the local authorities, particularly where (as the judge points out) the local authorities have never addressed the appropriate remedy for the dimensional zoning violation now established by the panel decision. No case holds that the judge may not solicit the view of the local authorities, and consideration of the local view would appear to be consistent with the case law. See Duteau v. Zoning Board of Appeals, 47 Mass. App. Ct. 664, 669-670 (1999), and cases cited therein. In any event, I do not believe intervention is required on an interlocutory basis here, particularly where the plaintiff's concerns can be raised, if appropriate, in any appeal from final judgment. The petition is accordingly denied. (Englander, J.)*Notice/Attest/Piper, J
