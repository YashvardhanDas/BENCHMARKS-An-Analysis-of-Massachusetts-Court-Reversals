09/27/2018 ORDER: The wife has filed a petition pursuant to G. L. c. 231, § 118, first par., seeking review of an order of the Dukes County Family and Probate Court ordering that real estate containing the marital home be sold. The property in which the home is located consists of three units, one unit being the marital home, and two rental units. During the divorce proceedings, the parties entered into a stipulation that was adopted by the court as a temporary order that wife and the only child of the marriage would remain in the marital home pending conclusion of the divorce proceedings. The husband agreed to make the mortgage and utility payments. The parties also agreed to share the rent from the units. Later in the proceedings, the husband sought a change in the temporary order such that the parties split equally the mortgage payments and the wife pay the utility and cable bills because the husband alleges that he is no longer being able to afford the carrying costs of the marital home. The judge denied this motion without prejudice.

Thereafter, the parties attempted mediation. Ultimately, the husband filed a motion to sell the entire property after the parties' mediation efforts were unsuccessful. The judge allowed the motion, citing in particular the length of time the matter has been pending and will take to conclude, the value of the assets and costs to the defendant to continue maintaining them, and the real estate market and uniqueness of the real estate at issue.

After a thorough review of the petition, the parties' submissions, and the judge's order, I can discern no abuse its discretion. The judge has the authority to issue temporary orders during the pendency of the divorce action, and those orders are reviewed for an abuse of discretion. See Fazio v. Fazio, 91 Mass. App. Ct. 82, 87-89 (2017). The judge explained his reasons for issuing the order, and the wife's attribution of other reasons to the judge are not credible. Accordingly, the petition is denied. (Ditkoff, J.). *Notice/Attest/Smola, J.
