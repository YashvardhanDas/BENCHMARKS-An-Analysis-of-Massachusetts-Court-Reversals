01/30/2019 RE#1: Denied. "Appellate review of a trial court order disposing of a preliminary injunction application, either by a panel of this court or by a single justice acting on a petition under the first paragraph of G. L. c. 231, Section 118, focuses on whether the trial court abused its discretion -- that is, whether the court applied proper legal standards and whether the record discloses reasonable support for its evaluation of factual questions. . . . [S]ince our commissions do not authorize us to sit as trial judges, we must exercise special care not to substitute our judgment for that of the trial court where the records disclose reasoned support for its action." Edwin R. Sage Co. v. Foley, 12 Mass. App. Ct. 20, 25-26 (1981). With respect to the central issue in any proceeding involving injunctive relief, likelihood of success on the merits, the parties have made competing representations concerning the basis of the judge's ruling. In the absence of a transcript of the hearing before the motion judge, and thus any record of his ruling, I am not in a position to say that the judge abused his discretion in allowing the motion, or that it lacked reasoned support.

In balancing the factors relevant to injunctive relief, the judge was permitted to take into consideration the fact that the relief granted was in the nature of prejudgment security, and to weigh irreparable injury accordingly. See generally Packaging Indus. Group, Inc. v. Cheney, 380 Mass. 609, 614 (1980). Cf. Rule 4.2, Mass.R.Civ.P. (permitting prejudgment trustee process upon a showing of likelihood of success). There was evidence in the documentary record that the defendant lacked sufficient funds to purchase supplies for the job, thus permitting the judge to conclude that an injunction prohibiting dispersal of the proceeds of the sale of the property was warranted. Finally, whether to require a bond is directed to the discretion of the court. See Rule 65 ((c), Mass.R.Civ.P. The judge did not abuse his discretion in making the implicit finding that a bond was not necessary, where the injunction required only that money be placed in an escrow account, and no other basis for requiring a bond has been articulated.

Nothing in this order should be construed as a ruling by me on the merits of this contested matter. (Sullivan, J.) *Notice/Attest/
