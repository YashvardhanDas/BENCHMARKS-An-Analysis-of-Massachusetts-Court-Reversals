04/03/2019 #13 Reservation and Report: " I have carefully reviewed the complaint, the defendants' motion to dismiss, and supporting materials. In my view, the case raises some important legal issues concerning the medical parole statute, G. L. c. 129, § 119A. Because the issues are substantial and recurring, I therefore reserve and report the case to the full court. The record before the full court shall consist of the pleadings and supporting materials filed in the county court, this reservation and report, and the docket sheet in SJ-2019-57. Further, the parties are to prepare and file in the full court a sufficiently comprehensive statement of agreed facts that will enable the court to resolve the legal issues raised in the pleadings. The failure to agree on all the facts needed for a decision could impair the court's ability to decide the case. In addition to any other issues, the parties shall address in their briefs: 1. Whether, for purposes of G. L. c. 127, § 119A, a written petition for medical parole of a prisoner must be considered by the superintendent of the facility where the prisoner is incarcerated, regardless of the superintendent's view as to the completeness or adequacy of the petition. 2. Which party bears the burden of preparing or procuring "(i) a medical parole plan; (ii) a written diagnosis by a physician licensed to practice medicine under section 2 of chapter 112; and (iii) an assessment of the risk of violence that the prisoner poses to society." G. L. c. 127, § 119A. 3. Whether the Commissioner of Correction, on receipt of the petition and the superintendent's recommendation as to release of the prisoner, must provide notice to the prisoner of the recommendation, as well as a copy of the recommendation and any supporting or related materials. This matter shall proceed in all respects in conformance with the Massachusetts Rules of Appellate Procedure. The plaintiffs are designated as the appellants.The parties shall confer with the Clerk of the Supreme Judicial Court for the Commonwealth regarding a schedule for the filing and service of briefs. The statement of agreed facts shall be finalized in time for inclusion in the record appendix filed with the appellants' brief. The pendency of this appeal should not preclude the parties from jointly working toward a resolution of this matter." (Cypher, J.)
