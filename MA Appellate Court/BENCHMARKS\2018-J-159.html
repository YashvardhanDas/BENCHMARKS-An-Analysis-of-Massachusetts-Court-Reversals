05/02/2018 RE#1: Husband has filed a motion pursuant to Mass.R.A.P. 6(a) for a stay of paragraph 34 of the Divorce Judgment, dated October 20, 2017, which awarded Wife weekly alimony in the amount of $7,325.11. A party seeking a stay pending appeal pursuant to Mass.R.A.P. 6(a), must demonstrate (1) the likelihood of success on the merits of the appeal; (2) the likelihood of irreparable harm if the stay is denied; and (3) the absence of substantial harm to the other party if the stay issues. See Packaging Indus. Group, Inc. v. Cheney, 380 Mass. 609, 617 (1980); Planned Parenthood League of Massachusetts, Inc. v. Operation Rescue, 406 Mass. 701, 710 (1990). Upon review of the papers submitted, Husband's motion for stay is allowed in part and paragraph 34 of the judgment is modified downward so that Husband shall hereafter pay Wife $3,508.00 per week in alimony pending the outcome of this appeal or until further order of this Court or a Single Justice thereof. This reflects the amount that Husband represented to the Probate and Family Court that he was able to pay in January 2018 when he sought a similar stay there. At the time of that filing, Husband had obtained new employment that paid him $225,000 per year, however this amount is still far short of the $1,180,259.70 in annual income attributed to him by the Probate and Family Court when alimony was determined. (Maldonado, J.). *Notice/Attest/Gorman, J.
