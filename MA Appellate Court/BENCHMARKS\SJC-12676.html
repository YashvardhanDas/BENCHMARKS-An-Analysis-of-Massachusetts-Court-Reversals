01/23/2019 #10 ANNOUNCEMENT: The Justices are soliciting amicus briefs. Where an employee alleges violations of the Wage Act, G. L. c. 149, §§ 148, 150, against multiple affiliated corporate entities, what test should the court apply to determine which of the entities, if any, face potential liability as an "employer" under the statute? Specifically, should the court apply the considerations set forth in G. L. c. 149, § 148B, to each alleged entity, as the plaintiffs argue; or, as the defendants argue and the judge below ruled, is liability under the Wage Act limited to one corporate employer (and its management), namely, that which issues the individual's paycheck, unless the plaintiff alleges sufficient facts to warrant "piercing the corporate veil"?

09/26/2019 #27 ORDER: This matter came before the Court on appellees' unopposed motion to postpone oral argument citing "robust settlement discussions" including mediation for two days in September. This appeal was entered in the Appeals Court on June 6, 2018, and was fully briefed on November 20. Following this Court's sua sponte transfer, it was entered here on January 16, 2019. The clerk's office, in addition to the October 1 oral argument date, attempted to schedule this case for argument in May and September but could not due to the unavailability of counsel. After the clerk sent notice to the parties of available October argument dates on August 6, and in the absence of any response to the notice from counsel for appellees, the Court issued its order for the October 1 oral argument on August 13. Upon consideration, the Court allows the motion to postpone argument with reluctance. This matter will be scheduled for argument in either November or December. The clerk may dispense with the customary notice of available argument dates and will issue an order for oral argument on one of the following dates: November 4, 5, or 7 and December 5, 6, or 9. Further postponement of oral argument will not be allowed. The parties shall file joint status reports concerning the progress of their settlement discussions on October 1, October 7, October 11, and October 15. By the Court.
