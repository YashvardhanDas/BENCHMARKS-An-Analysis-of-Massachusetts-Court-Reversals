03/29/2019 ORDER: Upon review of the assembly of the record transmitted from the Middlesex Superior Court, it appears that the notice of appeal docketed on 1/22/19 and purportedly from the "Court's decision allowing Defendants' motion to dismiss" is premature as no final judgment has entered onto the lower court docket. See Department of Revenue v. Mason M., 439 Mass. 665, 673 (2003) ("It is the entry of a final judgment, not the order found in the concluding paragraph of the judge's memorandum of decision, that starts the appellate clock."). The appellant is ordered to show cause in writing why jurisdiction over this appeal is proper or alternatively may take action in the Middlesex Superior Court to cause entry of judgment pursuant to Mass. R. Civ. P. 58(a) and 79(a). See Zielinski v. Connecticut Valley Sanitary Waste Disposal, Inc., 70 Mass. App. Ct. 326, 328-332 (2007). Upon entry of a judgment, the appellant may file a notice of appeal from that judgment. Mass. R. A. P. 4(a)(1). Appellate proceedings are stayed pending the completion of these steps or other response to this order. Status report due 4/19/19. *Notice/Attest.
