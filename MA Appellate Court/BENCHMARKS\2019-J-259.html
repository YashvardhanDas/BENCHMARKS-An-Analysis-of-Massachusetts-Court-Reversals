06/07/2019 RE#2: ALLOWED FORTHWITH. The petitioner's Affidavit of Indigency appears regular and complete on its face, and indicates that the petitioner is indigent. The court hereby allows waiver of the entry fee. Notice Sent.
06/07/2019 RE#1: Petitioner has filed with the single justice an "Appeal from Untimely Deadline for Payment of Appeal Bond and Use and Occupancy." She seeks review of the Housing Court's May 31, 2019 Order setting an appeal bond in this case and ordering her first payment due on June 1, 2019. General Laws c. 239, s. 5 governs such requests for review. Pursuant to the statute, a party may file a request for review with the clerk of the housing court "within 6 days after receiving notice of the decision of the court on the motion to waive the bond." G.L. c. 239, s. 5(g). Where review is sought pursuant to section 5, "no execution shall issue until 5 days from the date the defendant has received notice of the decision of the reviewing court." G.L. c. 239, s. 5(h). Accordingly, to the extent the petitioner has timely filed a request for review of the bond with the clerk of the housing court, no execution or finding of contempt shall issue pending this court's decision on the request for review of the bond. Now, the petitioner must await the transmittal of the assembly pertaining to her request for review of the bond from the clerk of the housing court to this court, which to date has not been received. Accordingly, this matter is dismissed as premature. A copy of petitioner's "appeal" filed on June 4, 2019 is hereby transmitted to the Housing Court pursuant to Mass. R. A. P. 4(a). (Rubin, J.) *Notice/attest/Horan, J
