02/27/2019 #4 MEMORANDUM AND ORDER: In this summary process action, the tenant unsuccessfully moved in Housing Court to stay her eviction. Before me now is her motion seeking such a stay. For the reasons that follow, I am constrained to deny the request for a stay.

The landlord commenced this proceeding in July of 2018. On August 16, 2018, the parties entered into an agreement for judgment under which the tenant agreed to vacate the premises by December 31, 2018. When that did not occur, the landlord moved for an execution. A Housing Court judge rejected the tenant's argument that the landlord himself was in breach of the agreement for judgment and granted the requested execution.

The defendant's motion for a stay pending appeal is not properly before me, as there is no appeal pending. In any event, the terms of the agreement for judgment requiring the tenant to vacate the premises by December 31, 2018, appear clear and unqualified.

To the extent the tenant seeks to challenge the underlying agreement for judgment, her claims fail because she waived her appellate rights in executing it. To the extent that the tenant seeks to challenge the issuance of the execution, she has not presented substantiated grounds for doing so.

Motion denied (Milkey, J.) *Notice/attest/del Puerto, J.
