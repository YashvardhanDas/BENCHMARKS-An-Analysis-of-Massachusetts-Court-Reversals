10/10/2018 #6 ANNOUNCEMENT: The Justices are soliciting amicus briefs. Whether a plaintiff alleging a violation of the Wage Act and regulations promulgated thereunder may bring a class action without satisfying the requirements of Mass. R. Civ. P. 23, as amended, 471 Mass. 1491 (2015), where G. L. c. 151, § 20, and G. L. c. 149, § 150, expressly provide that an aggrieved employee may bring an action "on his own behalf, or for himself and for others similarly situtated." Whether the complaint was wrongly dismissed as moot on the ground that the plaintiff had been afforded complete relief as to his individual claims, where the defendant tendered a settlement offer that the plaintiff did not accept.
