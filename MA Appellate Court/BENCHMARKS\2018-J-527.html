11/29/2018 ORDER: The defendant in this summary process case, Cheryl LeBlanc, filed a request for review of an appeal bond set by a judge of the Central Housing Court after a hearing. See G. L. c. 239, §§ 5 & 6.

Judgment for possession entered in favor of the plaintiff on August 28, 2018. The plaintiff filed a motion for the setting of an appeal bond, and LeBlanc filed a motion to waive the bond. The plaintiff's motion was allowed, LeBlanc's motion was denied, and the bond was set after a hearing on October 19, 2018. According to LeBlanc's request for review, she received formal notice of the bond order on that date. Under the statute, a request for review must be made within the time for filing notice of appeal -- that is, ten days from the entry of the judgment, see G. L. c. 239, § 5(a) -- or within six days of receiving the court's decision on the motion to waive the bond, whichever is later. See G. L. c. 239, § 5(g). As the time for filing the notice of appeal from the judgment had long passed, the six-day provision applied, and LeBlanc's request for review was due to be filed in the Housing Court on or before October 25, 2018. As her request for review was not filed until October 30, 2018, it was out of time, and the single justice has no jurisdiction to accept the request. Accordingly, the request for review must be dismissed.

Even if the request was properly before me, I would still be required to deny it. To waive the appeal bond, the Housing Court judge or the reviewing single justice must determine both that the defendant had a nonfrivolous defense to the judgment for possession and that the defendant is indigent. See Tamber v. Desrochers, 45 Mass. App. Ct. 234, 237 (1998). LeBlanc has not shown that she has defense to the judgment for possession that is not frivolous. "Defenses are frivolous if there is not reasonable expectation of proving the defenses alleged." Id. LeBlanc contends that the judgment was improper because the foreclosing bank never proved ownership of the note, and that she was never shown the note. However, I have reviewed the plaintiff's summary judgment motions in both this case (18H85SP003289) and in the prior summary process matter (17H85SP003377). A copy of the note is included in both, and the materials establish the foreclosing bank's possession of the note and ownership of the mortgage at the time of foreclosure. LeBlanc has not asserted a defense that is not frivolous. Accordingly, the request for review of the bond order is dismissed. LeBlanc must comply with the order within five days of her receipt of notice of this decision or risk the dismissal of her appeal. See G. L. c. 239, § 5(h). (Massing, J.). *Notice/Attest/Horan, J.
