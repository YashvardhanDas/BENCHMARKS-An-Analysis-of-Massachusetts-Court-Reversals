07/22/2019 #1 Appeal (Contains IMPOUNDED Affidavit of Indigency) entered pursuant to M.G.L.c. 261, § 27(D) with attachments.
07/23/2019 ORDER: Pursuant to G. L. c. 261, § 27D, the plaintiff appeals from the denial of her request for state payment of the transcription costs associated with proposed depositions of the parties and possible witnesses in this litigation. The plaintiff initially filed a request to waive the entry fee and for waiver, substitution, or state payment of other costs (paper #1). This document was not transmitted to this court by the Superior Court, and thus it is unclear, based on the record before me, whether the deposition costs were requested at this time and upon what basis the plaintiff claimed indigency. The Superior Court docket reads: "07/06/2018 1 Affidavit of Indigency and request for waiver substitution of state payment of fees and costs filed without Supplemental affidavit FILING AND SERVICE ONLY. Further ffindings [sic] on indigency necessary with respect to other requested relief." I presume that this means that the plaintiff requested waiver of the entry fee, waiver, substitution or state payment relating to service of the plaintiff's complaint, and waiver, substitution, or state payment of other fees. I further surmise from the docket entry that the clerk allowed the motion to waive the entry fee and the plaintiff's request relating to service, but neither allowed nor denied the remaining requests.

Subsequent to the 07/06/2018 entry on the Superior Court docket, there are several additional docket entries, including multiple filings and notations regarding hearings held or rescheduled, but none are obviously related to the plaintiff's request for state payment of costs until 06/12/2019. On that date, there is an entry showing that the plaintiff filed an ex parte motion to authorize payment of costs from the indigent persons funds. On 06/24/2019, the Superior Court entered the margin order denying the plaintiff's motion onto its docket. Based on the docket, it does not appear that any hearings occurred between 06/12/2019 and 06/24/2019. On 06/28/2019, the plaintiff filed a notice of appeal from the order denying her motion for state payment of the deposition costs.

The plaintiff's request for state payment of the costs of the depositions is governed by G. L. c. 261, §§27A-27D. The cost of transcribing a deposition is an extra cost. G. L. c. 261, § 27A. "If the court makes a finding of indigency, . . . it shall not deny any request with respect to extra fees and costs if it finds the document, service or object is reasonably necessary to assure the applicant as effective a prosecution, defense or appeal as he [or she] would have if he [or she] were financially able to pay. The court shall not deny any request without first hold a hearing thereon; and if there is an appeal pursuant to section twenty-seven D following a denial, the court shall, within three days, set forth its written findings and reasons justifying such denial, which document shall be part of the record on appeal." G. L. c. 261, § 27C (4).

In this case, based on the record before me, it does not appear that there was a hearing on the plaintiff's motion before it was denied. Moreover, subsequent to the filing of the notice of appeal, it does not appear that the required findings were set forth. No findings appear on the docket and none were transmitted by the clerk's office. Without those findings, it is impossible to determine whether the motion was denied because the judge found that the plaintiff was not indigent, because the judge found that all of the deposition transcripts were not reasonably necessary to assure the plaintiff as effective a prosecution as if she were able to pay, or both. Therefore, the order denying the plaintiff's motion for state payment of the cost of the deposition transcripts is vacated. The Superior Court shall either allow the motion or hold a hearing thereon as required by the statute. In the event of further appeal, the Superior Court shall enter the required findings before transmitting the appeal to the Appeals Court. So ordered. (Shin, J.). *Notice/Attest/Campo, J.
