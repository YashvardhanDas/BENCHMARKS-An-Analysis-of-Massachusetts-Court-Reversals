05/24/2018 ORDER: Joyce Currie seeks single justice review of an order (dated April 4, 2018) denying her motion to waive appeal bond and other costs. Co-defendant Bruce Duvelow does not join in this appeal. On appeal, the petitioner contends that she is indigent, has meritorious issues on appeal, and, in the alternative, that the use and occupancy amount should be reduced to $300 per month based on her ability to pay. After careful consideration of the papers and oral argument, I conclude on the record before me that the petitioner is indigent but that she has failed to present any nonfrivolous defense. See G. L. c. 239, s. 5. The judge accordingly did not err in denying the motion to waive bond pending appeal. That said, the judge made no findings and gave no rationale with respect to the amount of the use and occupancy he imposed, which appears to have been arrived at without submissions from either party. I note that the premises in question are, according to the owner, nonresidential and, accordingly, are of a sort for which no residential rent can be charged as a matter of law. The record before me does not show that the judge made a finding concerning the nature of the premises, including whether the premises are commercial. Nor does the record show whether the judge determined that a tenancy had ever been created. I do not mean to imply that the judge made no such findings, only that the record before me does not include them. If such findings have not already been made, then findings on these issues should be made on remand as part of the judge's reconsideration of the amount of use and occupancy, if any. I do not intend to imply that G. L. c. 239, s. 5 applies where no tenancy ever existed. If the judge finds that no tenancy ever existed, then the cause of action may lie in trespass rather than in summary process. For these reasons, I vacate that portion of the April 4, 2018 order imposing $1,500 monthly in use and occupancy payments, and remand for further proceedings on the issue of the appropriate amount of use and occupancy to be charged, if any. The order is in all other respects affirmed. I note that any party aggrieved by any further order under G. L. c. 239, s. 5 may seek single justice review. (Wolohojian, J.). Notice/Attest/Sullivan, J.
