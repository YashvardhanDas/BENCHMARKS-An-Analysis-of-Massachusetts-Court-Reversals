02/25/2019 #19 ORDER: (RE#10) Following a jury trial in Superior Court, the defendant was convicted of two counts of trafficking in heroin (one in an amount over 200 grams, the other in an amount over 100 grams). Before me now is the defendant's motion to stay execution of his sentence pending appeal. When a defendant seeks a stay of execution of sentence pending appeal, there are two categories of considerations that govern the exercise of the single justice's discretion: those relating to the likelihood of success on the merits of the appeal, and those relating to security. Commonwealth v. Hodge, 380 Mass. 851, 855 (1980); Commonwealth v. Levin, 7 Mass. App. Ct. 501, 504 (1979). I agree with the defendant that he does not appear to warrant security concerns or to pose a flight risk.[1] However, having considered the respective arguments made by the parties, I conclude that the defendant has not made a sufficient showing regarding a potentially meritorious appeal to warrant the issuance of the requested stay. Accordingly, I deny the motion. (Milkey, J.). *Notice/Attest.

Footnote

1. The defendant was released on pretrial bail without incident for over two years. In addition, while he has prior convictions, they are at this point decades old.
